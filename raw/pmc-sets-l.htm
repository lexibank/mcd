Note that Marshallese ḷaŋi-(n) has contrastive heavy ḷ where light l would be expected, with i in next syllable, whereas heavy ḷ is to be expected in the ḷaŋe-(n) of a popular saying. Why the second vowel of this latter form is lowered in not understood, but it provides an explanation for the heavy ḷs in the Marshallese ‘bad weather’ forms.
